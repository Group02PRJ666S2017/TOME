Tournament Types

There are 4 different types of tournaments that can be run as part of TOME:

  1. Single Elimination
  2. Double Elimination
  3. Round Robin
  4. Multilevel
Single Elimination

Single elimination tournaments are constructed as follows:

  • Competitors are set in pairings of two.
  • If there are an uneven amount of participants, some competitors will be paired with a "BYE", giving them an automatic round win.
  • The winner of each pairing will move on to the next round of the tournament.
  • If a competitor loses their round, they are eliminated from the tournament.
  • The tournament will continue until there is a single winner.
Double Elimination

Double elimination tournaments are constructed as follows:

  • There are two brackets in double elimination, UPPER and LOWER.
  • Competitors are set in pairings of two in the upper bracket of the tournament.
  • If there are an uneven amount of participants, some competitors will be paired with a "BYE", giving them an automatic round win.
  • The winner of each pairing will move to the next round of the tournament in the upper bracket.
  • The loser of the upper bracket will move to the lower bracket.
  • The lower bracket rounds play similarly to that of a single elimination tournament, where the winner moves on and the loser is eliminated.
  • The upper bracket is structured to maintain a similar round level as the lower bracket, which means at points the upper bracket will wait for lower bracket round completion.
  • The winner of the tournament is determined between the winner of the upper bracket and the winner of the lower bracket.
Round Robin - Requires a registered account

Round Robin tournaments are constructed as follows:

  • Round Robin tournaments have a maximum competitor amount of 32.
  • Competitors are set in pairings of two.
  • Competitors will play each other competitor in the tournament once.
  • Points are recorded in the standings of the tournament.
  • The competitor with the highest point total will win the tournament
  • Round robin tournaments can have additional stages added (see additional stage selection below).
Multilevel - Requires a registered account

Multilevel tournaments are constructed as follows:

  • The organizer sets the number of rounds for the stage. It is recommended that the number of rounds be approximately 30% of the total number of competitors, and not exceed the number of players minus one.
  • Competitors are set in pairings of two.
  • Competitors will be paired based on their current record.
  • Pairings are done to avoid having competitors play multiple times.
  • Points are recorded in the standings of the tournament.
  • The competitor with the highest point total will win the tournament.
  • Multilevel tournaments can have additional stages added (see additional stage selection below).

Creating a Tournament

To create a tournament follow the instructions listed below:

  1. Click the Create Tournament option in the left navigation bar on the main dashboard.
  2. Fill in the required fields for the tournament marked by the * symbol.
  3. Select the type of tournament to create.
  4. Select any additional stage options that may be required. The options are explained below.
Additional Stages

When selecting a Round Robin or Multilevel style tournament, there are some extra options to select from.

Organizers can run up to a total of 3 stages in a single tournament, combining multiple types of tournaments together.

For example, a user can run the following tournament combinations:

  • Round Robin Single Elimination
  • Multilevel Round Robin Double Elimination

Each of the options below define the number of competitors that are selected to move forward to the next stage of a tournament.

  1. No Cutoff
    • All competitors move on to the next stage of the tournament no matter their current standings at the end of the stage.
  2. Half Cutoff
    • The top 50% of competitors move on to the next stage based on their current standings at the end of the stage.
  3. Other
    • The organizer of the tournament must define the number of competitors that will move forward to the next stage.

The user is directed to the Tournament Summary page upon completion of creating a tournament.

Controlling Tournament Competitors

To add competitors to a tournament:
  1. Click the button in the bottom right corner or select Control Tournament Competitors from the left navigation bar
  2. Add competitors to the tournament
    • Competitors require a valid name and email
    • Competitors may be added individually or through a CSV file
    • If loading through a CSV file, column A should contain the name of the competitor and column B should contain the email of the competitor.
    • No header should be included in either column of the CSV file.
  3. Competitors may be added until the maximum competitor limit is reached that was defined during tournament creation.
  4. Competitors may be edited or deleted after being added to the tournament list.

The Role of Coordinators

Coordinators are individuals designated by the tournament organizer to have some administrative rights over a single tournament that has been created by the organizer.

Organizers are provided both a URL and coordinator password that is created uniquely for each tournament on the tournament summary page.

Coordinators can do the following within an assigned tournament:

  • Update a match result.
  • Start a tournament.
  • Start and end a round.
  • Add/remove competitors.

League tournaments do not have coordinators and therefore all League tournament results must be controlled by the League organizer.

Tournament Standings

Users can view tournament standings at any point during a tournament.

Points are awarded to competitors as determined by the following rules:

  • 3 points to the winner of a match.
  • 0 points to the loser of a match.
  • 1 point in the case of a tie.

Points are used as a measure to determine who moves on if additional options are selected during tournament creation.

Creating a League

Requires a registered account to access any league functionality.

To create a league follow the instructions listed below:

  1. Click the Create League option in the left navigation bar on the main dashboard.
  2. Fill in the required name field for the league on the main dashboard view

The user is directed to the League Dashboard page upon submission of a league name.

Controlling Competitors in a League/League Tournament

There is a maximum limit of 128 competitors that can be added to a League.

To add competitors to a league:
  1. Select Control League Competitors from the left navigation bar
  2. Add competitors to the League
    • Competitors require a valid name and email
    • Competitors may be added individually or through a CSV file
    • If loading through a CSV file, column A should contain the name of the competitor and column B should contain the email of the competitor.
    • No header should be included in either column of the CSV file.
  3. Competitors may be added until the maximum competitor limit is reached.
  4. Competitors may be edited or deleted after being added to the league.
To add competitors to a league tournament:
  1. Click the button in the bottom right corner or choose Select Tournament Competitors from the left navigation bar.
  2. Add competitors to the tournament by selecting enroll or withdraw next to each competitor.
  3. Competitors may be added until the maximum competitor limit is reached that was defined during tournament creation, or until the maximum limit of the league has been reached.

Creating a League Tournament

To create a tournament in a league follow the instructions listed below:

  1. Ensure that competitors are loaded into the league competitor pool.
  2. Click the Create League Tournament option in the left navigation bar on the league dashboard.

League and League Tournament Standings

League Tournament Standings

Users can view tournament standings at any point during a tournament.

Points are awarded to competitors as determined by the following rules:

  • 3 points to the winner of a match.
  • 0 points to the loser of a match.
  • 1 point in the case of a tie.

Points are used as a measure to determine who moves on if additional options are selected during tournament creation.

League Standings

Users can view tournament standings at any point from the league dashboard.

League standings are similar to that of tournament standings, but also list the following:

  • Tournaments Played - shows the number of tournaments a competitor has played within the league
  • Rating - shows the rating used to calculate more efficient match pairings in future tournaments
